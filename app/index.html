    N early 15 years ago, our founder, Hank “Hip” Jones, discovered his passion. From his basement in Decatur, Hip entertained neighbors and friends, stirring flavorful alcoholic concoctions and preparing delicious meat and vegetarian dishes. Hip so enjoyed hosting these gatherings every week, he decided to bring his bartending and culinary prowess to the masses.

    In 2005, he opened his first pop-up restaurant on the outskirts of Decatur. Each night, folks from across metro Atlanta packed Hip’s place, ordering everything from Prosciutto to Paprika Cured Pork Loin. But it wasn’t just the food that was a hit. Hip’s drinks, from the classic Tango Martini Cocktail to his famous Mint Martini, delighted faithful patrons — while fetching thousands of dollars in revenue.

    Word of Hip’s pop-up spread, and soon his location was too small for the burgeoning crowd of 20-, 30-, and 40-somethings. In March 2013, Hip leased a 20,000-square-foot space on Decatur’s East Court Square. Hip hired an eager crew of chefs, waiters, and bartenders, even luring employees away from perennial iconic restaurants such as Rathbun’s and Sundial. Then he expanded his menu, serving up seafood bisque and hog chops, for instance, and he stocked his bar with artisanal craft beers and other drink favorites.

    Hip & Stir was born -- to rave reviews.

    In October 2013, Atlanta Journal-Constitution food critic John Kessler wrote that Hip & Stir “is a breath of fresh air in a sea of culinary sameness.” Patrons often remark that although Hip & Stir is an upscale food-and-drink venue, its appeal lies in the fact that it lacks the pomp and pretentiousness of restaurant competitors in Midtown, Virginia-Highland and Buckhead.

    Could we see more Hip & Stir locations in the future?

    “Perhaps,” says Hip, “but for right now, I’m just focused on making this location the swankiest spot for good food and good libations in Decatur.”
